# Summary: Trader Behavior vs Market Sentiment

## Methodology

The analysis integrates market sentiment data with trader activity to understand behavioral and performance patterns. Data was cleaned, aligned on a daily level, and transformed into meaningful metrics such as PnL, trade frequency, and directional bias.

---

## Key Insights

1. Trader profitability is higher during Fear phases, indicating better opportunities during volatile markets.
2. Traders increase activity and position size during Fear, while showing reduced engagement during Greed.
3. High-leverage traders outperform low-leverage traders, highlighting the role of risk-taking.
4. Profitability is weakly correlated with individual features, suggesting multi-factor dependency.

---

## Strategy Recommendations

* During Fear: Increase participation with controlled risk.
* During Greed: Reduce exposure and avoid overconfidence-driven trades.
* Segment-based strategies should be applied for different trader types.

---

## Conclusion

Market sentiment significantly influences trader behavior and performance. Adaptive strategies based on sentiment can improve trading outcomes.
